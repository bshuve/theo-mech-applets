Along with a visualization of the particle's motion along a specified path, we now show various quantities (such as the kinetic and potential energies, and their derivatives) as a function of time. We also show the integral of several of these quantities over the entire path, plotted as a function of \(p\), the parameter used to specify the path. The checkboxes allow you to choose which of these integrated quantities to plot. Recall that the alternative path is parametrized by the function \(y(t) = (1+p)(1-t^2)\). For simplicity, we are assuming that motion is occurring on the Planet Ilton where \(g=2\).
