Newtonian vs. Lagrangian Framework

(Chrome Recommended.)

Using the Pig on the Dome problem as a framework, the graphs demonstrate \(F_N\) vs. theta (\(\theta\)) and time (\(t\)). The left-hand-side graphs use the Newtonian method to solve for \(F_N\), and the right-hand-side graphs use the Lagrangian method to solve for \(F_N\). Mass (\(m\)), radius of the dome (\(R\)), and the gravitational constant (\(g\)) are set to 1.0 \(kg\), 1.0 \(m\), and 9.8 \(\frac{m}{s^2}\) respectively. There is a provided slider for the graphs, \(\theta_i\) for the initial theta value in the system. Here, the lowest initial energy possible is \(mgRcos(0.001)\), where theta inital \(\theta_i\) starts at 0.001, so that the pig is able to slide down the dome still.
