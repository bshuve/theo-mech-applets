üåÄ What is the Brachistochrone Curve?

The Brachistochrone Problem asks:

What is the shape of the curve down which a particle will slide from one point to another (under gravity and without friction) in the least time?

The answer, surprisingly, is a cycloid ‚Äî the path traced by a point on the rim of a wheel as it rolls along a straight line. This solution was first proposed by Johann Bernoulli in 1696 and led to the development of the calculus of variations.

üìê Mathematical Representation

The parametric equations of a cycloid are:

\[ x(t) = r(t - \sin t), \quad y(t) = r(1 - \cos t) \]

Here, \( r \) is the radius of the rolling circle, and \( t \) is the angle of rotation (in radians). As \( t \) increases, the point traces out the cycloid horizontally along the x-axis.
