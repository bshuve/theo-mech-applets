Newton's 2nd Law and Least Action

This applet explores the connection between Newton's 2nd Law and the Principle of Least Action, showing graphically that the path minimizing the action is also the only path which satisfies Newton's 2nd Law.

Consider an object of mass \(m=1\) \(kg\) constrained to move in the \(y\)-direction under uniform gravitational acceleration \(-g\), where the gravitational constant \(g=9.8\) \(m/s^2\). The kinetic energy of the object is \(T=\frac{1}{2}m\dot{y}^2\) and the potential energy is \(U=mgy\). We consider the motion of the object from \(t=-\sqrt{\frac{2y_i}{g}}\) s to \(t=\sqrt{\frac{2y_i}{g}}\) s, with its position beginning and ending at \(y=0\). The position \(y_i=1\) \(m\) occurs when \(t=0\) \(s\).

In the applet below, we consider a family of paths which add a cosine time dependence to the classical trajectory. The cosine function is chosen so that the initial and final positions are the same as the classical path. Specifically, the path is parametrized by the function \(y(t) = (y_i-\frac{1}{2}gt^2) + py_i\cos(\frac{\pi}{2}\frac{t}{t_0}) \) so that when \(p=0\), we have the classical path \(y(t) = y_i - \frac{1}{2}gt^2\).



Answer: The acceleration is \(-g-p(\frac{\pi}{2}\frac{1}{t_0})^2\cos(\frac{\pi}{2}\frac{t}{t_0}) \)F=ma only when p = 0.
